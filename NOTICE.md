## Attribution & lineage

The theology-of-preaching frame in this workflow — "something always happens", the four works every text seeks to accomplish, *syniēmi* / "standing under", and the critique of modernist application pressure — is a **synthesis of**, and draws direct quotations from:

> Darrell W. Johnson, *The Glory of Preaching: Participating in God's Transformation of the World* (IVP Academic, 2009).

`frameworks/johnson-methodology.md` is our own notes and synthesis of that work for the purpose of commentary and study; it is **not** a substitute for the book. If this framework is useful to you, **read the book** — Johnson's argument is richer than any synthesis. The quotations are reproduced as short excerpts for commentary and attribution.

This workflow's *structure* (Constitution-as-supreme-law, staged artifact handoffs, a framework reference layer, an adversarial Critic) is adapted from a general-purpose AI writing-workflow pattern, stripped down for low-volume, single-author use.
