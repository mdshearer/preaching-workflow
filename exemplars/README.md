# Exemplars

Worked examples — **texture, not formula**. Read them to see how the method actually runs end-to-end and what "good" feels like in a finished reflection. Do not copy their structure as a template; copy the *discipline*.

## What's here

- **`EXAMPLE-luke-13-set-free.md`** — one fully-**fictional** reflection through all seven stages, by a made-up preacher ("Robin") whose voice is defined in `../frameworks/EXAMPLE-voice-profile.md`. It shows:
  - what honest Devotional notes look like (not an essay);
  - exegesis kept in service of the four works;
  - a unified message stated in under ten words and how it's derived from the text;
  - a homiletical draft written for the ear;
  - a Critic pass that scores voice *and* theology — including catching the **self-help drift** (Constitution Part A) mid-development and naming the fix;
  - the final cut, shorter than the draft.

Everything in it is invented. Replace it — and the example voice profile — with your own as you build a body of real reflections worth keeping in view.

## As you accumulate your own

When one of *your* delivered reflections is worth learning from later, you can drop a copy (or a pointer) here. Keep the set small: an exemplar earns its place by teaching something the frameworks don't.
