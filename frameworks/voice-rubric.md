# Voice Rubric

*Purpose: the 100-point scoring instrument used by the Critic stage. Each category is scored independently with examples from the draft; total score yields the verdict band (90+ Exceptional, 80–89 Strong, 70–79 Good, 60–69 Developing, <60 Major rework). Pair with the theological completeness check (the four works) — a draft can score well here and still fail theologically.*

*Where a category refers to "the preacher's idioms" or "authentic voice", measure against `voice-profile.md` — the specific, exemplified profile, not a generic ideal.*

---

**Total Points: 100**

## 1. Conversational Authenticity (15 points)

**Measures:** Natural language, relatable tone, friend-to-friend communication.

**Excellent (13–15 points):**
- Uses natural, unpolished language — the preacher's own profiled idioms (see `voice-profile.md`)
- Feels like conversation with a trusted friend
- Avoids academic or overly formal register
- Includes appropriate humour and colloquialisms

**Good (10–12 points):**
- Generally conversational with occasional formal lapses
- Mostly accessible language with minor academic intrusions
- Some natural expressions but inconsistent

**Needs Development (6–9 points):**
- Mix of conversational and formal tone
- Some stilted or overly polished sections
- Accessible but lacks distinctive voice

**Poor (0–5 points):**
- Predominantly formal or academic tone
- Lacks natural speech patterns
- Feels distant or impersonal

## 2. Vulnerable Transparency (15 points)

**Measures:** Personal disclosure, honest struggle sharing, authentic weakness.

**Excellent (13–15 points):**
- Shares specific personal struggles without spiritual polish
- Admits mistakes, failures, and ongoing challenges
- Reveals genuine uncertainty and doubt
- Shows real consequences and emotions

**Good (10–12 points):**
- Some personal sharing with minor spiritual buffering
- Generally honest but occasionally sanitised
- Shows struggle but may lack specific details

**Needs Development (6–9 points):**
- Limited personal disclosure
- Tends toward safe or resolved struggles only
- Vulnerability feels calculated or incomplete

**Poor (0–5 points):**
- No meaningful personal disclosure
- Maintains safe pastoral distance
- Avoids genuine struggle or uncertainty

## 3. Narrative Theology Integration (12 points)

**Measures:** Personal story as theological vehicle, parallel discovery, story-driven interpretation.

**Excellent (11–12 points):**
- Personal stories carry equal weight to biblical exposition
- Natural (not forced) parallels between ancient and modern
- Story becomes valid form of biblical commentary
- Integration of narrative and theology feels effortless

**Good (8–10 points):**
- Good story integration with minor forced connections
- Stories support but don't carry theological weight
- Generally natural parallels

**Needs Development (5–7 points):**
- Stories feel illustrative rather than interpretive
- Some forced or superficial connections
- Narrative and theology feel separate

**Poor (0–4 points):**
- Stories are purely anecdotal
- No meaningful theological integration
- Forced or inappropriate parallels

## 4. Interactive Community Engagement (10 points)

**Measures:** Question-driven content, space for response, community discernment elements.

**Excellent (9–10 points):**
- Ends with meaningful questions rather than answers
- Creates genuine space for community response
- Trusts the congregation to make connections
- Includes multiple engagement methods

**Good (7–8 points):**
- Some interactive elements with minor prescriptive tendencies
- Generally question-oriented with occasional answer-giving
- Limited variety in engagement methods

**Needs Development (4–6 points):**
- Few interactive elements
- Tends toward providing answers rather than questions
- Minimal community engagement opportunities

**Poor (0–3 points):**
- Purely monologue format
- No space for community response
- Preacher-centred rather than community-oriented

## 5. Structural Clarity and Flow (10 points)

**Measures:** Logical progression, clear transitions, coherent organisation.

**Excellent (9–10 points):**
- Clear, logical progression from introduction to conclusion
- Smooth transitions between ideas and sections
- Easy to follow structure that serves content
- Timing and pacing well-managed

**Good (7–8 points):**
- Generally clear structure with minor confusion
- Most transitions work well
- Mostly easy to follow

**Needs Development (4–6 points):**
- Some structural issues or unclear progression
- Awkward transitions or jumps
- Occasionally hard to follow

**Poor (0–3 points):**
- Unclear or chaotic structure
- Poor transitions
- Difficult to follow logic

## 6. Practical Theological Grounding (10 points)

**Measures:** Real-world application, contextual interpretation, practical discipleship.

**Excellent (9–10 points):**
- Clear connections between biblical text and modern life
- Practical applications without being prescriptive
- Addresses real-world complexity and nuance
- Balances spiritual and practical concerns

**Good (7–8 points):**
- Generally practical with some abstract elements
- Good modern connections with minor gaps
- Mostly addresses real concerns

**Needs Development (4–6 points):**
- Some practical elements but tends abstract
- Limited real-world application
- May oversimplify complex issues

**Poor (0–3 points):**
- Predominantly abstract or theoretical
- Little practical application
- Disconnected from real-world concerns

## 7. Authentic Voice Consistency (8 points)

**Measures:** Consistent tone, genuine personality, avoiding performative spirituality.

**Excellent (7–8 points):**
- Consistent authentic voice throughout
- Genuine personality shines through
- Avoids spiritual performance or false piety
- Voice feels natural and unforced

**Good (5–6 points):**
- Generally consistent with minor lapses
- Mostly authentic with occasional performance
- Voice feels mostly natural

**Needs Development (3–4 points):**
- Inconsistent voice or frequent lapses
- Some performative elements
- Voice feels somewhat forced

**Poor (0–2 points):**
- Inconsistent or inauthentic voice
- Heavily performative
- Voice feels artificial or imposed

## 8. Hope and Complexity Balance (8 points)

**Measures:** Realistic hope, acknowledging difficulty, avoiding oversimplification.

**Excellent (7–8 points):**
- Balances genuine hope with honest difficulty
- Acknowledges complexity without despair
- Avoids both false optimism and cynicism
- Shows faith as journey rather than destination

**Good (5–6 points):**
- Generally balanced with minor extremes
- Mostly realistic about difficulty and hope
- Some oversimplification

**Needs Development (3–4 points):**
- Tends toward false optimism or excessive pessimism
- Oversimplifies complex issues
- Limited nuance in addressing difficulty

**Poor (0–2 points):**
- Extreme optimism or pessimism
- Significant oversimplification
- Lacks realistic assessment

## 9. Engagement and Interest (7 points)

**Measures:** Listener retention, compelling content, varied engagement methods.

**Excellent (6–7 points):**
- Consistently engaging and interesting
- Uses variety of methods to maintain attention
- Content feels compelling and relevant
- Natural rhythm and pacing

**Good (4–5 points):**
- Generally engaging with some flat spots
- Limited variety in engagement methods
- Mostly interesting content

**Needs Development (2–3 points):**
- Inconsistent engagement levels
- Repetitive or monotonous sections
- Some boring or irrelevant content

**Poor (0–1 points):**
- Generally unengaging
- Little variety or interest
- Difficult to maintain attention

## 10. Self-Deprecating Wisdom (5 points)

**Measures:** Humble positioning, learning alongside congregation, avoiding expert pose.

**Excellent (5 points):**
- Positions self as fellow learner, not expert above
- Admits uncertainty and ongoing growth
- Shows God working despite human limitation
- Humble without false modesty

**Good (3–4 points):**
- Generally humble with minor expert positioning
- Some admission of uncertainty
- Mostly learning alongside congregation

**Needs Development (1–2 points):**
- Occasional expert positioning
- Limited admission of uncertainty
- Some distance from congregation struggle

**Poor (0 points):**
- Consistent expert positioning
- Claims special insight or authority
- Lacks humility or learning posture

---

## Scoring Guide

- **90–100:** Exceptional — distinctive voice firing on all cylinders
- **80–89:** Strong — clear authentic voice with minor areas for development
- **70–79:** Good — solid foundation with several areas needing attention
- **60–69:** Developing — shows potential but needs significant work
- **Below 60:** Needs major development — missing key elements of authentic voice

## Usage Notes

- Score each category independently first, then review for overall coherence.
- Look for patterns across categories (e.g. high authenticity but low engagement).
- Consider audience and context when applying the rubric.
- Use specific examples from the text to justify scores, and measure "voice" against `voice-profile.md`.
- Focus on development areas with highest impact potential.
- This rubric measures *voice*. A reflection scoring 90+ on voice can still fail theologically — pair with the four-works check in `johnson-methodology.md`.
