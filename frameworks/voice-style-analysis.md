# Voice & Style Analysis — the instrument

*Purpose: the instrument for capturing a preaching voice. Used during onboarding to build `voice-profile.md`, and re-read at the start of the Devotional stage to orient a reflection before any text comes near the page. This file is the **blank tool**; `voice-profile.md` is the filled result for a specific preacher; `EXAMPLE-voice-profile.md` shows a fictional filled example.*

---

## What a voice profile is

A voice profile names the handful of characteristics that make your preaching sound like *you* and nobody else, **each pinned to concrete examples of your own phrasing**. The examples are the whole point. "Conversational" is an adjective anyone could claim; *"the way you say 'I hadn't a clue what to do' instead of 'I found myself uncertain'"* is enforceable — the Critic can hold a draft against it.

A profile has two parts:

1. **Voice characteristics** (3–5) — how you sound.
2. **Theological & structural tendencies** — how you build a reflection.

## How to capture it (the method onboarding uses)

Don't write a voice profile from imagination. Build it from **evidence**: two or three samples of how you actually write or speak — a past talk transcript, a blog post, a long voice note, an email you're proud of. Read the samples for:

- **Recurring idioms** — the phrases you reach for. Note the *actual words*.
- **Register** — where do you sit between casual and formal? Where do you slip into a "performance" register (pious, academic, corporate) that isn't really you?
- **What you're willing to disclose** — what kind of personal material do you actually share, and how specifically?
- **How you handle a story** — does your personal experience carry the meaning, or just illustrate it?
- **How you end** — questions? imperatives? a landing image?

## The dimensions to define

For each **voice characteristic**, write: a one-line name, two or three sentences describing it, and **at least two concrete example phrases** in your own words. Strong starting dimensions (adapt, rename, or replace):

- **Conversational authenticity** — sounds like you talking to a friend, not writing a document. Contractions, your real vocabulary, natural rhythm. *Avoids the academic register and the pious register equally.*
- **Vulnerable transparency** — specific personal struggle, concrete and ongoing, not tidily resolved. Calibrated by detail: if a listener can't picture the struggle day-to-day, it hasn't landed.
- **Narrative theology** — personal story as real biblical commentary, not decoration. The story carries weight; the parallel to the text is discovered, not engineered.
- **Self-deprecating wisdom** — fellow learner, not expert above. Admits uncertainty. Humble without performing humility.

For **theological & structural tendencies**, capture things like:

- **Experience vs information** — do you favour participatory discovery or content delivery?
- **How you bridge** ancient text to now — what kinds of parallels feel honest in your mouth?
- **Interactivity** — discussion, pair-sharing, silence, multi-sensory elements?
- **How you close** — the kind of question or image you trust to do the work.

## The bar (what makes a characteristic usable, not decorative)

- It has **named example phrases**, not just an adjective.
- A draft could **fail** it — you can imagine prose that violates it. (If nothing could violate it, it isn't doing any work.)
- It's **yours** — it would not equally describe ten other preachers.
- The Critic can **point at a line** in a draft and say "this honours it" or "this breaks it".

When your profile clears that bar for every characteristic, save it as `voice-profile.md`. That file is now the reference the Constitution's Refusals and the Critic's rubric measure against.
